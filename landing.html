MISSION OBJECTIVE

ISRO's Geostationary satellites operate in a harsh radiation environment. Energetic particle fluxes of electrons at geostationary orbit pose a significant threat to spacecraft electronics, causing deep-dielectric charging and operational anomalies. Our mission is to forecast these hazards.

TRANSFORMER TECHNOLOGY

Leveraging state-of-the-art Time Series Transformer architecture with an encoder-only design (3 layers, 4 heads, d_model=64) to capture complex temporal dependencies in solar wind parameters and historical flux. Trained on 8 years of GOES/Wind mission data.

  • Sequence length of 72 (6 hours)
  • Attention-based feature extraction
  • Multi-horizon prediction heads

GSAT CONSTELLATION

Positioned at 74°E Longitude (Altitude 35,786 km), the GSAT constellation forms the backbone of India's telecommunications. By predicting the energetic electron environment at L=6.6 Re, we safeguard these multi-million dollar national assets from space weather events.
